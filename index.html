Pune, the second largest city in Maharashtra, is known by many names and has a rich history, culture, and identity: The Oxford of the East: Pune is known for its many educational institutions, including universities, colleges, and management schools. The cultural capital of Maharashtra: Pune is known for its rich history and cultural activities, including classical music, theater, sports, and literature. The Queen of the Deccan: Pune is known by this nickname. The Detroit of India: Pune is known for its strong presence in the automobile sector. Home to the Serum Institute of India: Pune is home to the world's largest vaccine manufacturer. Home to the Aga Khan Palace: Pune is home to the Aga Khan Palace, a majestic building that served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi and others during the Indian freedom movement. A green city: Pune has more than 40% of its area under green cover. A smart city: Pune is one of 109 cities shortlisted in India's Smart Cities Mission

Pataleshwar Caves

The Pataleshwar Caves, also referred to as the Panchaleshvara temple or Bhamburde Pandav cave temple, are an 8th-century rock-cut Hindu temple from the Rashtrakuta period located in Pune, Maharashtra, India. Dedicated to Shiva, it was a monumental monolithic excavation with a notable circular Nandi mandapa and a large pillared mandapa.

Sinhagad Fort

Sinhagad is an ancient hill fortress located at around 49 km southwest of the city of Pune, India. Previously known as Kondhana, the fort had been the site of many battles, most notably the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670.
